Welcome to Avadepth

Avadepth - is distributed weekly to river pilots, Port of Vancouver, and shipping companies to assist them in determining the maximum draft and the best sailing times to transit the Fraser River.

The computerized reporting system predicts draft availability and the corresponding "transit window" for deep sea ships to navigate the South Arm Main Channel of the Fraser River between Sand Heads (kilometre 0) and Fraser Surrey Docks (kilometre 34).

About Avadepth - Background & Theory of Operation

Avadepth Reports

CCG Weekly Report

An eight day forecast of available water depths for the Fraser River South Arm Navigation Channel; the weekly report is published every Friday for the coming week. The calculated water depths are displayed in a time-series graph for the selected period; the forecasts are generated by the Canadian Coast Guard, Western Region using the computer program AVADEPTH. See "Background & Theory of Operation" for addition information.

Daily Depths

Graphs the available water depths for a selected date; and identifies the location and depth of the critical control point (local name and chainage) for each two-hour period; relative to the current sounding, tidal influence and river flow measured at Fraser River station (08MF005) Hope BC.

User defined variables are selected from the Parameters drop-down menu.

Transit Window

Displays available "transit window" for a selected period (day/week/month) using two approaches:

  • Determines available hours for transit by specifying a required "depth"; or
  • Determines the maximum depth for transit by specifying a required "transit window" to reach your destination i.e. Fraser Surrey Docks.

User defined variables are selected from the Parameters drop-down menu.

Predicted Water Levels & Velocities

Displays predicted water levels and velocities every two kilometres along the Fraser River in a tabular format. User defined variables are selected from the Parameters drop-down menu.

These are pre-generated water levels and velocities from a one-dimensional hydrodynamic model; that was employed as a general predictive tool for assessing hydrodynamic conditions on the river. By assigning tidal elevations at the Strait of Georgia, and river discharges upstream, a water level and average current can be computed for each cross section defined in the model domain. The model computations were on a 10 minute time interval and simulated nine different river flow / discharge scenarios.

The selected approach utilized a combinationw of 1-D and 2-D numerical models of the river in order to optimize model performance. The water level data that was generated by the 1-D model was subsequently utilized to define the upstream boundary conditions in the 2-D model runs for the "Animated Currents".

Animated Currents

Displays animated currents within predefined reaches of the Fraser - South Arm Channel. User defined variables are selected from the Parameters drop-down menu.

These are pre-generated currents from a two-dimensional hydrodynamic model which represents a depth average flow condition through the water column at a pre-defined location; allowing the user to assess if flow conditions are either strong, moderate, or weak and in which direction.

Users need to be aware that during periods of low river flow, fresh water may flow over the salt water at depth and create stratified conditions; for this reason, when comparing predicted values to either the surface currents or the currents at depth there may be some significant differences. Comparisons of the model results to an acoustic current metre located at Annieville Channel showed a general accuracy of +/- 10%.

It should be noted that large, deep vessels navigating the river channel experience currents throughout much of the water column and, as such, depth-averaged currents should provide a reasonable indication of overall current-induced effects on the vessel.

Fraser River Hydrograph

Illustrates the seasonal variations of flow conditions for the Lower Fraser River; and will aid the user in selecting the appropriate river discharge when calculating the available water depths. Each graph is colour coded to represent both actual and predicted flow rates, as well as a maximum and minimum range of historical flow conditions representing approximately 100 years of recorded data from the Water Survey of Canada, Real-Time Hydrometric Station for Fraser River at Hope, BC.


Channel Bottom Conditions

Fraser River – South Arm Channel Condition Report

A weekly printout of channel bottom conditions for the Fraser River Deep Sea Navigation Channel; the report references the least soundings and available channel width at each kilometre for the inner (one-lane) and outer (two-lane) channel limit; date of the survey, reference to survey drawing / plan, and the design channel grade at each kilometre.

Fraser River – South Arm Channel Overview

A composite of the most current soundings (surveys) of the main navigation channel from Sand Heads (km -1.0) to New Westminster (km 35.0). The soundings are referenced to CHS Chart Datum (Local Low Water) and colour coded to indicate when they were last updated, for example: less than 7days old, between 7 to 30 days and more than 30 days. The water depths have also been contoured and colour banded relative to the channel design grade of 11.5 metres.

Fraser Surrey Docks – Berth Soundings

A composite of the most current soundings (surveys) of the berths and approach channels to Fraser Surrey Docks. The soundings are referenced to CHS Chart Datum (Local Low Water) and colour coded to indicate when they were last updated, for example: less than 7days old, between 7 to 30 days and more than 30 days. The water depths have also been contoured and colour banded relative to the channel design grade.

Survey Drawings

Access to a repository of channel monitoring surveys, that allows you to view and download drawings of main commercial channels monitored by the Canadian Coast Guard, Waterways Management.

Selection is made by using a number of filters from the Parameters drop-down menu or by selecting (clicking on) a defined waterway or river reach from the Reference Map.

Users will need to install an Autodesk DWF viewer to display and view the survey drawings.

Channel Infill & Scour Analysis
